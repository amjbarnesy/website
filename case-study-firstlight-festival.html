First Light Festival by Adam

Every year since 2019, on the most easterly beach in the UK. Six years of festival photography for First Light: from the beach at sunrise to the wings of the stage, backstage portraits, press calls, pavilion performances and events across the county.

Client First Light Festival
Ongoing since 2019
Services Festival Photography · Event Photography · Portraits · Press Calls

The brief

First Light Festival is one of those events where the setting does half the work and the photographer has to keep up with the other half. A free beach festival on the Suffolk coast, timed around the summer solstice, with music, arts and community at its core. The brief from the start was to document it properly: not highlights reel photography, but images that actually capture what the event feels like to be at.

That means being there when the light is extraordinary and the crowds are still thin, and being equally useful three hours later when the stages are full and the atmosphere has shifted entirely. It means knowing when to step back and let a moment breathe, and when to get close enough that you can feel the energy in the frame. The same photographer covers the beach at dawn, the backstage corridors, the press calls and the pavilion programme. Consistency of eye across all of it is what makes the work useful across print, social and press.

Since 2019 the commission has grown beyond the main festival to include other events across the county, giving First Light a body of photography that reflects the full breadth of what they do, not just the headline weekend.

The work

Festival photography works when the photographer stops being a visitor with a camera. After six years, the rhythms of First Light are familiar: where the light falls on the beach at a particular hour, which moments are worth waiting for, which performances reward being close rather than back. That knowledge shows in the images.

Coverage across the years has included the festival beach at sunrise, main stage and acoustic performances, backstage access and artist portraits, press calls, the pavilion programme and atmospheric work that feeds into their wider communications. Additional events around Suffolk have extended the archive beyond the festival itself.

The images are used across First Light's print materials, social channels and press output. When other organisations ask First Light for a photographer recommendation, they put Adam's name forward first. That is the kind of outcome you work six years to earn.
